Homework Policy & Assignments

General:
  • Homework will be collected at the beginning of nearly every class.  Homework assignments and due dates (subject to change) can be found at the bottom of this webpage, and should be checked daily.
  • Quizzes may be given without advance notice, but will usually be announced at least one day in advance.  A quiz may occasionally replace the homework that was due on a given day, in which case the questions will be drawn directly from that homework or from similar problems.
  • Class participation is strongly encouraged!  Each unit, up to 3 points of extra credit for attendance may be added to a student's exam score from that respective unit. To receive this extra credit, a student's participation must be noted as regular, which is to say that he/she:
    • asks/answers questions in class
    • corrects mistakes
    • goes to the board to work on problems
    • comes to class and stays awake!

Format:

  • A student's name should appear at the top of each page of his/her homework.
  • All pages must be stapled together, and the paper may not be torn from a spiral notebook so as to leave frayed edges. 
  • Please write legibly to ensure accurate grading!

Exceptions:

  • Late homework/quizzes will not be accepted. 
  • Rare exceptions may be made only if the student has contacted me in advance to make other arrangements. 
  • The lowest homework or quiz score from each unit will be dropped

Grading:

  • Each homework assignment is worth 15 points.  Three problems will be graded on accuracy (worth up to 3 points each).  The remaining problems will be graded on completion.  That is, the 15 possible points are separated into 9 possible points for accuracy and 6 possible points for completion.
  • Each quiz is also worth 15 points.  Every quiz problem will be graded on accuracy, and the point-value of each problem will vary according to the total number of problems on the quiz.
  • All homework and quiz scores from each of the four units will be totaled and scaled to 50 points.  Thus, a maximum total of 200 points on homework and quizzes throughout the semester is possible.
