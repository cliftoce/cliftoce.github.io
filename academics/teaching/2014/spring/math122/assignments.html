  • Part 1 (worth up to 10 points). Choose one of the problems that you answered incorrectly on the midterm.   • Part 2 (worth up to 15 points). Select a concept that you studied as part of your preparation for the Midterm, but which did not appear on the exam. Explain your understanding of this concept and provide at least one illustrative example. The example may not be one of the examples from the notes/textbook, but rather should be one of the following: (i) an exercise that was not assigned as homework, (ii) an example that you look up online, or (iii) a problem you make up on your own. Clearly identify which of these options you choose for your example (i.e. if you choose a textbook exercise, state which one; if you find an example online, cite the source; if you make up the problem, simply say so).
  • Both parts of this assignment should be presented together in the form of a short paper (it may be handwritten, if well-organized and legible). Your score will be based on the depth of knowledge demonstrated in following the above instructions. The more detail you put into your explanations and examples, the higher your score will be.

Sample Exam 3.
