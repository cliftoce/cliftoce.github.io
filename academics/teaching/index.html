Teaching:


As a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Mathematics from Fall 2010 through Spring 2014, I held sole responsibility of classroom instruction for various math courses at KU. The following is a list of my former teaching assignments.





Past Courses:


  • Math 002 - Intermediate Algebra.
    Semesters: Fall 2010, Spring 2011.
    Description: Math 002, a second level algebra course, is the lowest level mathematics course offered at the University of Kansas. Students not prepared for Math 101 will be permitted to enroll in Math 002. However, before enrolling in Math 002, such students are encouraged to prepare by self-study or by completing a beginning algebra course in high school, community college, or correspondence study.
    Text: Intermediate Algebra: A Graphing Approach, Martin-Gay & Greene, Pearson Custom Publishing, 2008.

  • Math 104 - Trigonometry and Precalculus.
    Semester: Fall 2011.
    Description: An introduction to the elementary functions (polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric) and their properties. Intended primarily for students intending to enroll in MATH 121.
    Text: Precalculus: Functions and Graphs, 5th Ed., Larson, Hostetler, Edwards, Brooks/Cole, 2011.

  • Math 115 - Business Calculus I.
    Semester: Spring 2012.
    Description: Elementary differential and integral calculus, with applications in management and the biological sciences.
    Text: Applied Calculus, 9th Ed., Tan, Brooks/Cole, 2012, University of Kansas edition.

  • Math 121 - Engineering Calculus I.
    Semesters: Fall 2012, Fall 2013.
    Description: Differentiation and integration of algebraic and trigonometric functions. Applications to physical sciences and engineering.
    Text: Calculus: Concepts and Contexts, University of Kansas Fall 2012 Edition (similar to 4th Ed.), Stewart, Brooks/Cole, Prentice Hall.

  • Math 122 - Engineering Calculus II.
    Semester: Spring 2014.
    Description: Sequences and series. Calculus of functions in polar and spherical coordinates. Three-dimensional vector geometry and vector-valued functions. Multivariable calculus: partial derivatives and multiple integration.
    Text: Calculus: Concepts and Contexts, University of Kansas Fall 2012 Edition (similar to 4th Ed.), Stewart, Brooks/Cole, Prentice Hall.





Other Duties:


  • Served as the Summer Director of the Kansas Algebra Program (KAP).
    Semester: Summer 2011.

Specific duties included coordinating three sections of Math 002: Elementary Algebra and five sections of Math 101: College Algebra, supervising and evaluating their respective undergraduate teaching assistants, preparing all course materials (including syllabi and exams) for over 100 students, and implementing the MyMathLab online courseware system for homework and quizzes.
