Math 115 – Spring 2012

Homework Policy & Assignments

General:
  • The course is divided into four units, with an exam marking the conclusion of each.
  • Homework will be collected regularly.  Homework assignments and due dates (subject to change) can be found at the bottom of this webpage, and should be checked daily.
  • Quizzes may be given without advance notice, but will usually be announced at least one class day in advance. Students may expect 2 quizzes per unit.
  • Class attendance is required. Each unit, up to 15 attendance points will be awarded, for a total of 60 possible points throughout the semester. If a student is unable to attend class due to illness or a medical/family emergency, he/she should contact the instructor immediately to arrange for an excused absence. Each student is permitted one unexcused absence without penalty per unit; beyond that, each unexcused absence will result in the deduction of 5 attendance points up to the maximum of 15.
  • Participation is strongly encouraged! A student's participation will be noted if he/she regularly:
    • asks/answers questions in class
    • politely draws attention to and/or corrects mistakes
    • goes to the board to work on problems as requested
    • comes to class and stays awake!

Format:

  • A student's name should appear at the top of each page of his/her homework.
  • All pages must be stapled together, and the paper may not be torn from a spiral notebook so as to leave frayed edges. 
  • Please write legibly to ensure accurate grading!

Exceptions:

  • Late homework/quizzes will not be accepted. 
  • Rare exceptions may be made only if the student has contacted the instructor in advance to make other arrangements. 
  • The lowest homework score from each unit will be dropped

Grading:

  • Each homework assignment is worth 15 points.  Three problems will be graded on accuracy (worth up to 3 points each).  The remaining problems will be graded on completion.  That is, the 15 possible points are separated into 9 possible points for accuracy and 6 possible points for completion.
  • For each unit, a student's homework scores will be totaled and scaled to 25 points.  Thus, a maximum total of 100 points on homework throughout the semester is possible, accounting for 10% of the final grade.
  • Each quiz is worth 5 points.  Every quiz problem will be graded on accuracy, and the point-value of each problem will vary according to the total number of problems on the quiz.
  • For each unit, up to 10 quiz points are possible. Together with the 60 possible points for attendance (see above), the combined quiz/attendance score is out of 100 points and thus accounts for another 10% of the final grade.

Reference materials:

  • Gateway exam problems. These are not assigned nor collected, but they are worth taking a look at and starting to practice.
  • Graphing calculator basics. Read this if you are unfamiliar/unexperienced with the TI-83/84 calculators.
  • Symbols used in Math 115. These will be introduced throughout the semester. If you ever forget what a particular symbol represents, look it up here.

Your score, out of 10 possible points, will be assigned according to the depth of knowledge demonstrated in following the above instructions. The more detail you put into your explanations and examples, the higher your score will be. Conversely, I will know if an assignment was thrown together in a matter of minutes, and such submissions will receive low scores.
