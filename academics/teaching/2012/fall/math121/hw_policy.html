Homework Policy:


General:

  • The course is divided into four units, with an exam marking the conclusion of each.

  • Homework will be collected regularly. Assignments and their due dates are subject to change and should be checked daily.

  • Students are encouraged to seek answers to most of their questions during the instructor's office hours, since only a limited number of homework problems will be reviewed during class meetings.

  • Bonus quizzes may be given without advance notice, but will usually be announced at least one class day in advance. Students may expect one such quiz per unit.

  • Class attendance is an important component of success in the course! Each unit, up to 10 bonus points will be awarded for attendance. If a student is unable to attend class due to illness or a medical/family emergency, he/she should contact the instructor immediately to arrange for an excused absence. A student is permitted one unexcused absence per unit without penalty; each additional unexcused absence will incur a deduction of 5 bonus attendance points out of the possible 10.

  • Participation is strongly encouraged! A student's participation will be noted if he/she regularly:
    • asks/answers questions in class

    • politely draws attention to and/or corrects mistakes

    • goes to the board to work on problems as requested by the instructor

    • comes to class and stays awake!

Format:

  • A student's name should appear in the top right corner of each page of his/her homework, and should be written in the form "last name, first name."

  • All pages must be stapled together, and the paper may not be torn from a spiral notebook so as to leave frayed edges. Note that simply folding the corners of several pages together is not an acceptable substitute for the use of a staple.

  • Please write legibly to ensure accurate grading!

Exceptions:

  • Late homework/quizzes will not be accepted.

  • Rare exceptions may be made only if the student has contacted the instructor in advance to make other arrangements.

  • The lowest homework score from each unit will be dropped.

Grading:

  • Each homework assignment is worth 15 points. Three problems, each worth up to 3 points each, will be graded on accuracy. The remaining problems will be graded on completion. That is, the 15 possible points are separated into 9 possible points for accuracy and 6 possible points for completion.

  • For each unit, a student's homework scores will be totaled and scaled to 25 points. Thus, a maximum total of 100 points on homework throughout the semester is possible, accounting for about 12% of the final grade.

  • Each bonus quiz is worth 5 points. Every quiz problem will be graded on accuracy, and the point-value of each problem will vary according to the total number of problems on the quiz.

  • For the entire semester, totals of 20 bonus quiz points and 40 bonus attendance points (see above) are available. These 60 possible bonus points have the potential to raise a student's final grade by over half a letter, and thus should not be taken lightly!

Reference materials:

  • Gateway exam problems. These are not assigned nor collected, but they are worth taking a look at and starting to practice.

  • Graphing calculator basics. Read this if you are unfamiliar/unexperienced with the TI-83/84 calculators.

  • Symbols used in Math 121. Most of these will be introduced throughout the semester. If you ever forget what a particular symbol represents, look it up here.
