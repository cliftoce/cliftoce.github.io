Headphones:


I build open-back headphones with lathe-turned wooden sound chambers and low-impedance Turbulent X drivers. My latest-and-greatest pair is made out of bubinga wood, while earlier prototypes used black walnut and ebony.

I use Mogami W2893 Miniature Quad Microphone Cable for all wiring, including a custom-built detachable cable that plugs into the right headphone chamber via stereo 3.5mm ("mini") TRS connector.

A small Bluetooth receiver mounted on the headband provides the option to conveniently make the headphones wireless. While this works very well in many cases, the inherent latency of Bluetooth causes it to be ill-suited for movie-watching, where synchronicity between audio and video is vital.

These headphones produce unbelievable realistic sound, thanks not only to high-quality components but to their open-back design, which allows for a large soundstage. They are designed exclusively for in-home use, as they fail to block ambient noise whatsoever, but that's just fine since I prefer IEM's for on-the-go listening.
