Overview:


My journey as a photographer began in 2005 when I took my first digital camera, the chunky 2MP point-and-shoot Canon A40, on a four-month trip to Kenya and Tanzania. Enthralled by the amazing wildlife of East Africa, I quickly became hooked on photographing it. I took the same camera to Australia in 2006 and enjoyed capturing many more memories of foreign flora and fauna.

Some years later, I upgraded to a new point-and-shoot, the Canon A710-IS, and my first DSLR, the Nikon D40. These traveled with me back to Kenya in 2009, as well as to various locations in the states.

I have an underwater housing for my point-and-shoot and have taken it on a hundred-foot SCUBA dive, among other aquatic adventures.

Nowadays, I primarily shoot a Nikon D7000. For information about my lenses, etc., refer to the complete list of my photography equipment.

I have photographed two weddings (once as a hired contractor and once as a gift to friends) and, during two years at Whitman College, I was a photographer for the Whitman Magazine.

Check out my Sample Gallery for some older photos and my Flickr photostream for recent work. More photos will be posted soon!
