Cody E. Clifton received his Ph.D. in Mathematics from The University of Kansas (KU) in 2015. His doctoral research, under the supervision of Professor Bozenna Pasik-Duncan, focused on parameter estimation and adaptive control techniques applied to a stochastic system model for PageRank.

From 2012 to 2015, Cody was a Graduate Research Assistant, supported through the Information and Telecommunications Technology Center (ITTC) by the joint grants of Professors Tyrone Duncan and Bozenna Pasik-Duncan. From 2010 to 2014, he was a Mathematics Graduate Teaching Assistant, serving as the sole instructor of various undergraduate math courses at KU; in this capacity, he achieved recognition as a recipient of the 2013 Outstanding GTA Award and the 2014 Florence Black Award for Excellence in Teaching.

Cody was involved in the leadership of multiple student groups throughout his time at KU. He served as President of the Mathematics Graduate Student Organization, President of the KU Graduate Student Chapter of the AMS, and President of the KU Ki-Aikido Club.
